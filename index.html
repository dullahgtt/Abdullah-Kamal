Who am I?

Biography,
 a few words...

Hi, I'm Abdullah Kamal, a Machine Learning researcher whose main interest is in machine learning applications for changing the world (I know... it's corny). My research experiences range from machine learning applications to algorithms for developing safety nets for drone delivery systems to working on applicational neural networks for speaker diarization, more commonly known as speaker recognition. I have extensive experience in software engineering and research through my various technical positions. I have interned at Dell and worked research positions at Texas A&M-Corpus Christi and Texas State University, collaborating with faculty from various research institutions, leading to multiple publications through IEEE and ACM.

I also have extensive leadership experience through leading technical organizations. I led the Google Developer Student Club (GDSC) chapter at Texas State University as Vice President, where I led machine learning workshops and collaborated with industry partners from leading tech companies. Through GDSC, I co-founded the CS Launchpad, to empower diversity and inclusion in the various technical majors at TXST. I co-founded the Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics (SIAM) at Texas State, sitting on the Board as National Liaison. Through my work at SIAM, I had the honor of directing the first ever Julia Robinson Math Festival, leading to community outreach and support for the university. I also co-founded the Boko Hacks hackathon, partnering with Microsoft Federal, IEEE, CodePath, and KC7, bringing 200+ attendees from various universities. I have also held positions on the Boards of SME, SSE, and .EXE.
